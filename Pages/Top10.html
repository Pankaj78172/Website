1. S-400 "Triumf" – Russia

Price: $300–$400 million per unit

The S-400 is one of the most advanced and expensive air defense systems available. The system includes multiple components such as radar stations, launchers, and command posts, which contribute to the high price tag. A full S-400 system typically includes around 8–10 launchers with several radars and command units.

2. Iron Dome – Israel

Price: $50–$100 million per battery

Each Iron Dome battery includes a radar system, a command and control unit, and up to 20–30 launchers. The interceptors themselves cost around $40,000 per missile, and a typical battery consists of several launchers. The cost per interception can be high, but the system has a remarkable success rate (over 90%) in intercepting short-range projectiles.

3. THAAD (Terminal High Altitude Area Defense) – USA

Price: $800 million–$1 billion per system

A complete THAAD system includes the radar, launchers, interceptors, and command and control systems. The cost can vary depending on the configuration, but a single THAAD system with its radar and several launchers typically costs over $800 million. The interceptors cost approximately $100,000–$150,000 per missile.

4. Akash Missile System – India

Price: $20–$25 million per unit

India's Akash missile system is considered one of the more affordable surface-to-air defense systems. The cost of a single Akash battery (which includes several launchers and radars) is roughly $20–$25 million. The missiles themselves cost around $200,000 per unit, making it a cost-effective solution compared to other air defense systems.

5. C-RAM (Counter Rocket, Artillery, and Mortar) – USA

Price: $50 million–$100 million per system

The C-RAM system, often deployed to protect military bases from incoming artillery and rockets, costs around $50 million to $100 million depending on the configuration and the type of weaponry included. This includes the radar system and automated countermeasures. The Phalanx CIWS component used in C-RAM also costs around $15 million per unit.

6. RAPIDFire – France

Price: $25–$30 million per unit

The RAPIDFire CIWS system is priced around $25–$30 million. It provides high-velocity protection against fast-moving aerial threats like missiles, drones, and aircraft. It integrates radar, infrared sensors, and an automated 40mm cannon to intercept targets. This is a relatively cost-effective solution for modern naval defense.

7. Phalanx CIWS – USA

Price: $15–$20 million per unit

The Phalanx CIWS is widely used on naval ships to protect against missile and aircraft threats. The system costs around $15 million to $20 million per unit, depending on configuration. The price includes the radar system, a 20mm Gatling gun, and the associated software and control systems.

8. Barak 8 – Israel/India

Price: $50–$80 million per battery

The Barak 8 missile defense system, developed jointly by Israel and India, costs around $50–$80 million for a complete battery (including radar, launchers, and interceptors). Each missile costs approximately $100,000 to $150,000, with the system being able to intercept a variety of airborne threats at distances of up to 100 km.

9. Active Protection Systems (APS) – Various Manufacturers

Price: $1–$5 million per vehicle

Active Protection Systems like Trophy (Israel) and Iron Fist (Israel) for armored vehicles cost around $1 million to $5 million per vehicle depending on the configuration. These systems include radar, sensors, and countermeasures to destroy or neutralize incoming anti-tank missiles and RPGs. The cost depends on the vehicle and the complexity of the APS system installed.

10. Stinger Missile System – USA

Price: $50,000–$100,000 per missile

The Stinger missile, a man-portable air defense system (MANPADS), typically costs between $50,000 and $100,000 per missile. The cost can vary based on the version and quantity purchased. Stinger systems are widely used for short-range defense against low-flying aircraft and helicopters.
